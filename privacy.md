# Baseline Privacy Policy

**Last updated: May 14, 2026**

Baseline ("the app") is built and maintained by Ben Gibson. This page explains what data the app handles, how it's stored, and what we do — and don't do — with it.

## TL;DR

- Your body composition data stays on your device and in your personal iCloud account.
- Apple operates the iCloud sync; we never see your data.
- We collect no analytics, no advertising data, no tracking data.
- The app uses no third-party SDKs.
- There are no accounts. No sign-in. No server we operate.

## What data Baseline handles

Baseline lets you record body composition information — weight, body fat percentage, muscle mass, and related measurements. You enter this data manually, or by scanning an InBody printout with your device's camera. All photo recognition (OCR) happens entirely on your device; photos are never sent off the device for processing.

You may optionally provide your name, height, and age in the app. These fields are not required.

## Where your data is stored

**On your device.** All data is written to a local database on your device, protected by iOS file encryption.

**In your iCloud private database.** If you are signed into iCloud, Baseline syncs your data through Apple's CloudKit service to your private iCloud account. Apple encrypts this sync; we cannot access it. Your data is shared only between your own devices signed into the same iCloud account.

**To Apple Health (optional).** If you grant permission, Baseline writes your weight and body composition to Apple Health so other Health-aware apps can read it. Baseline never reads data from Apple Health.

## What Baseline does not do

- We do **not** operate any server that stores your data.
- We do **not** collect analytics, usage data, or telemetry of any kind.
- We do **not** use third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs.
- We do **not** track you across other apps or websites.
- We do **not** sell, share, or transmit your data to anyone.
- The app contains no advertising.

Apple separately provides aggregate, anonymous, opt-in usage data to developers via App Store Connect (downloads, crashes, retention). This data is collected and controlled by Apple, not Baseline. You can opt out in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics & Improvements.

## CSV export

You can export your data as a CSV file from within the app. This uses the iOS system share sheet — the file goes wherever you choose to send it (Files, Mail, AirDrop, etc.). Baseline does not retain or transmit this export.

## Data deletion

To delete your data:

- **In the app:** Settings → Reset offers a complete data wipe.
- **In iCloud:** iOS Settings → [your name] → iCloud → Manage Account Storage → Baseline → Delete Data.
- **From Apple Health:** Health app → Profile → Apps → Baseline → Turn Off & Delete.

Uninstalling the app removes the local copy of your data; the iCloud copy persists in your account until you remove it via iCloud settings.

## Children

Baseline is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from them.

## Changes to this policy

If we change how the app handles data, we will update this page and the "Last updated" date at the top. Substantive changes will also be noted in the app's release notes.
